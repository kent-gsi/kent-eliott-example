1 2 January 1923 Henry Eliot
  • Of course I must thank you for your goodness in writing so often, and keeping me posted about finance in America, and for your last admirable letter.
  • I think I shall return it to you to invest for me in America, as I do not like sinking any more money in England at the present juncture.
  • I am in sympathy with his views, in general, and he is one of the very richest and most powerful men in England
  • I could only come to New York and meet her at the boat, and leave (I don’t know whether there is any passport or other red tape to interfere).
  • If mother could not come – and if you think she should not come – I would come to America; but I have always felt that a visit of four or five days, such as mine must be limited to, would be more pain than pleasure to mother.
2 [Received 17 April 1924] His Mother
3 22 August 1925 Henry Eliot
4 24 June 1926 Henry Eliot
  • I have just returned from Paris, where I had a very busy and tiring week.
  • I am much more protected in London, and it is a real rest to be here, however busy one is.
  • I could make periodical visits to England; for in any case I think she had better stay out of England for a year or so

1 2 January 1923 Henry Eliot
  • In spite of the fact that I have been absent about ten or eleven weeks during the past year, owing to Vivien’s illness, and constantly late, they have raised my salary to five hundred pounds, at a time when they are raising very few salaries indeed.
  • I have been working toward getting a position in journalism under Lord Rothermere.
  • We have been keeping on our flat, and Ellen, simply because we felt that mother would not want to come to any other place
  • I should put her in to Clarence Gate to look after mother, with Ellen to cook, and Miss Duff could go about with mother and would be a much better person than Marion
  • Marion could thus get some holiday out of it, and perhaps could go into the country for a bit with Vivien.
